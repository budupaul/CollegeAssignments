Simple arithmetic instructions

In this lab you will learn how to use some simple arithmetic instructions.

1. Instruction syntax:
ADD d, s
d = d + s
- sums the operands and stores the result in the destination
- both operands must be the same type, e.g. byte or word (why?)
- at least one operand should be a register or a constant

INC d
d = d + 1
- the destination may be a byte or a word and is considered unsigned
- does not set the carry flag!

SUB d, s
d = d - s
- substracts the source operand from the destination operand and stores the result in the destination
- both operands must be the same type, e.g. byte or word
- at least one operand should be a register or a constant

DEC d
d = d - 1
- the destination may be a byte or a word and is considered unsigned
- does not set the carry flag!

NEG d
d = 0 - d
- operand may be a byte or a word
- obtain the 2's complement of destination operand

MOV d,s
d = s
- copies a byte or word from the source into the destination

CBW
AL -> AX
- converts the byte from AL into a word stored in AX, i.e. extends the sign bit of the AL register into the AH register


Obs. there are no instructions for unsigned conversions.
An unsigned conversion from byte to word is done by "moving" 0 into the higher byte of the word
e.g. mov ah, 0h ;AL -> AX
